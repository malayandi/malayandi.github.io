Research

Economics
  • Worked under Ulrike Malmendier to investigate whether past macro-economic and bank-specific shocks experienced and survived by a bank affect its current capitalization and risk-taking.
  • Bouwman, Christa H. S. and Ulrike Malmendier. 2015. "Does a Bank's History Affect Its Risk-Taking?." American Economic Review, 105(5): 321-25. PDF

Projects

An Analysis of Crime in Berkeley
  • For our Stat 133 final project, we wrangled and cleaned raw data on criminal incidents in Berkeley before critically mining the data, with the goal of identifying the safest area in Berkeley (My room had just been burgled at the time and I was looking for a new place to stay). We then created a Google Earth visualisation of the data for users to visually scan recent criminal activity in their area. PDF / Github / Google Earth
Linear Algebra Package (for Java)/ Matrix Calculator
  • Developed a basic linear algebra library for Java capable of executing fundamental matrix and vector operations efficiently (relatively speaking). I used this library to create a "Matrix Calculator" program as a teaching aid for UC Berkeley's Math 54 (Linear Algebra and Differential Equations), for which I was teaching the support lecture for at the time. Other Work

Theology
  • Following some severe confusion about the course-enrollment system at UC Berkeley, I ended up taking a course in Hindu Mythology in my first semester at Berkeley. In this course, I wrote a research paper on the relationship between the two primary antagonists in the Hindu Epic, the Mahabharata, using primary source material. This paper was published in the Berkeley Student Journal of Asian Studies.
  • Palaniappan, Malayandi. 2015. "The Friendship of Karna and Duryodhana in the Mahabharata." Berkeley Student Journal of Asian Studies, 1(5): 77-91. PDF
